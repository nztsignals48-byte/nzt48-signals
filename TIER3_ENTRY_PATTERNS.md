## Entry Type A: DIP RECOVERY (Oversold Reversal)

**When it happens:** Stock crashes 3-5% intraday, finds support, bounces
**Your role:** Buy the dip, ride the recovery

### Recognition Signals:
- ✅ RSI <30 (severe oversold, statistical reversal likely)
- ✅ Volume drops OR stabilizes (capitulation, support forming)
- ✅ Lower lows but improving momentum (RSI bottoming)
- ✅ RVOL spike on recovery attempt (volume confirms bounce)
- ✅ Time: >2 hours to session close (enough time for 30-60min hold)

### Example (SNDK):
```
09:30 UTC: SNDK opens at 645 (post-earnings spike)
09:45 UTC: Flash crash to 580 (RSI 15, volume panic)
10:15 UTC: Stabilization at 585 (RSI rising to 28, volume holding)

ENTRY: 585 (buy the dip, RSI <30, recovery volume)
EXIT: 620 (+6.0%, £3,000 on £10k position)
HOLD: 45 minutes
```

### Psychology:
- "Price has fallen 9.2%. It's extreme. Statistical mean reversion likely."
- "Volume is no longer selling off. Support is forming."
- "RSI at 28. Risk/reward favors long."

### Position Size: 2% (Tier 3 max)
### Stop Loss: 3% below entry (tight, defend against further weakness)

## Entry Type B: EARLY RUNNER (Momentum Catch) ← YOUR EDGE

**When it happens:** Volume EXPLODES before RSI gets extreme. Stock is breaking out.
**Your role:** Get in EARLY, BEFORE the extremes form, ride the momentum wave

### Recognition Signals:
- ✅ RVOL spike >2.5x (real volume, not noise, momentum building)
- ✅ Price in upper half of daily range BUT RSI 35-50 (NOT yet overbought)
- ✅ Volume bar larger than previous 20 bars (structural break)
- ✅ **Key:** Entry happens EARLY, before RSI >70
- ✅ Time: >2-3 hours to session close (riding the wave takes time)

### Example (SNDK):
```
11:00 UTC: SNDK trading sideways at 610, RSI 52, RVOL 1.0x (quiet)
11:15 UTC: News catalyst (earnings beat)
11:20 UTC: Buyers rush in
         Price: 620 (not extreme yet, RSI 58, but RVOL 3.2x)

ENTRY: 620 (EARLY, before RSI extremes)
      → You're not buying the dip (price is up)
      → You're catching momentum EARLY
EXIT: 660+ (ride until overbought, +6.5%+)
HOLD: 90 minutes (letting the wave play out)
```

### Psychology (THIS IS YOUR PATTERN):
- "Volume just spiked 3x. Something is happening."
- "Price is moving up but RSI isn't extreme yet. Early."
- "Get in early, ride the momentum to the overbought zone, then exit."
- NOT: "Wait for RSI >70 then buy" — That's late.
- INSTEAD: "Get in at RSI 40-50 when volume explodes, catch the ride up"

### Why This Is Your Edge:
- Most traders **wait** for RSI >70 to confirm (too late, missing the wave)
- You **catch early** when RVOL explodes, riding BEFORE extremes
- You exit as RSI approaches 75-80 (selling into strength)
- Result: 6-8% gains on volatile stocks before they exhaust

### Position Size: 2% (Tier 3 max)
### Stop Loss: 5% below entry (allow for momentum, but defend against reversal)

## Entry Type C: OVERBOUGHT FADE (Reversal Scalp)

**When it happens:** Stock spiked 5-8%, now at RSI >75, volume cooling
**Your role:** Short the strength, scalp the pullback

### Recognition Signals:
- ✅ RSI >75 (extremely overbought, exhaustion risk)
- ✅ Volume declining on the spike (divergence: price high, volume low = weakness)
- ✅ Price at resistance or session high (no room to go)
- ✅ RVOL still >1.5x (enough liquidity to exit)
- ✅ Time: >2 hours to session close (30-45 min hold is fine)

### Example (SNDK):
```
14:00 UTC: SNDK rallied to 660 (RSI 78, but volume drying up)
14:15 UTC: Trying to push higher but volume stalling
14:30 UTC: Profit-taking begins (buyers exhausted)

ENTRY: 660 SHORT (sell overbought, volume divergence)
EXIT: 620 (6.0% profit on pullback)
HOLD: 45 minutes
```

### Psychology:
- "Price is at 660, RSI is 78. Overbought."
- "Volume is declining. Buying power exhausted."
- "Pullback is likely. Scalp the fade."

### Why This Works:
- Overbought + volume divergence = mean reversion probability
- You're not fighting the trend (still in Tier 3 volatile)
- You're just catching the pullback phase within the bounce

### Position Size: 2% (Tier 3 max)
### Stop Loss: 5% above entry (if it keeps ripping, exit with small loss)
